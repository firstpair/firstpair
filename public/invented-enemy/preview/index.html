About this book

A history of ideas about Russia and the Slavs — the recorded encounters from Byzantium in 860 to the present, the hostile tropes they produced, each trope's mutations across five centuries, a formal ontology of twelve trope families, a five-chapter survey of Europe's literary mirror in all its major languages, the West's need for the enemy and the Russian mirror that answers it, and the modern accusation of "Russophobia" treated as a chapter in the history it names. Verified claims are asserted with sources; unverified details are visibly flagged.
