## About the book

A history of *ideas about* Russia and the Slavs, not of Russia itself: every major recorded encounter from the Byzantine homilies of 860 and Ibn Fadlan's Volga journal of 922 through the Muscovy travelogues, the Bowes and Macartney embassies, the Enlightenment, British Russophobia, race science, the Serbian case study, the Cold War, and the present — plus a five-chapter survey of the literary mirror in English, French, German, Slavic, and Nordic letters, and a final part on why the West needs the enemy, the Russian mirror of self-contempt and nationalist counter-accusation, and the double standard. The book extracts the recurring hostile tropes, traces each one's mutations across five centuries, formalizes them as an ontology of twelve trope families, and treats the modern accusation of "Russophobia" as itself a chapter in the history it names. Claims that passed a three-vote adversarial verification are asserted; everything else is flagged.

The full edition (twenty chapters and an interlude in thirteen parts, three appendices, ontology, six research dossiers, complete bibliography and prompt log) lives in the book's source repository, which owns the manuscript, metadata, versions, and build logic; this site hosts only the public preview package.
